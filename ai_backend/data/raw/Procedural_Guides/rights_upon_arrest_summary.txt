SUMMARY OF RIGHTS AND POLICE DUTIES UPON ARREST (CODE OF CRIMINAL PROCEDURE, 1973)

A person arrested without a warrant has these key rights:

Right 1: GROUNDS OF ARREST
The arrested person must be told why they are being arrested (Section 50(1)).

Right 2: RIGHT TO BAIL
If the offence is bailable, the person must be informed about their right to bail (Section 50(2)).

Right 3: MAGISTRATE PRESENTATION
The person must be presented before a Magistrate within 24 hours of arrest (excluding travel time) (Section 57).

Right 4: INFORMATION TO RELATIVE
Police must inform a friend or relative nominated by the arrested person about the arrest and detention place (Section 50A).

Right 5: RIGHT TO LEGAL AID
The person has a right to consult a lawyer during interrogation (Section 41D).
