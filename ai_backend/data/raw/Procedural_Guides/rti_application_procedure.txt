PROCEDURE FOR FILING AN RTI APPLICATION AND FIRST APPEAL (RIGHT TO INFORMATION ACT, 2005)

Any citizen can request information from a public authority under the RTI Act.

Step 1: WRITE THE APPLICATION
Write your RTI request in English, Hindi, or the official local language. Address it to the Central or State Public Information Officer (CPIO/SPIO) of the concerned department.

Step 2: PAY THE FEE
Attach the prescribed RTI fee (usually â‚¹10) via IPO, Demand Draft, or online payment.

Step 3: WAIT FOR RESPONSE
The PIO must reply within 30 days (48 hours if it concerns life or liberty). 

Step 4: FILE A FIRST APPEAL
If no response is received or information is denied, file a First Appeal within 30 days to the First Appellate Authority (an officer senior to the PIO).

Step 5: SECOND APPEAL
If the First Appeal is not resolved satisfactorily, a Second Appeal can be filed with the Central or State Information Commission within 90 days.
