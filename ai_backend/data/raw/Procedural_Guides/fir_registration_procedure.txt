PROCEDURE FOR FILING A FIRST INFORMATION REPORT (FIR) (CRPC, 1973)

An FIR is the first step in initiating criminal investigation.

Step 1: APPROACH POLICE STATION
Visit the nearest police station having jurisdiction over the area of offence.

Step 2: DESCRIBE INCIDENT
Give the details of the crime, date, time, place, and names (if known) of the accused.

Step 3: RECORDING OF FIR
The officer-in-charge must record your statement in writing and read it back to you for accuracy before you sign.

Step 4: GET A COPY
You are entitled to a free copy of the FIR immediately after registration (Section 154).

Step 5: FOLLOW-UP
If the police refuse to register the FIR, you can send the written complaint to the Superintendent of Police or file it before the Magistrate.
